Cost Breakdown: Tiny House

A typical tiny house on wheels costs between $40,000 and $80,000 for a professionally built 200-400 sq ft model. DIY tiny house kits start around $15,000 but require months of labor, tools, and construction knowledge.

Hidden costs of tiny houses:

  • Land: Tiny houses on wheels need an RV park or private land with hookups
  • Zoning: Many counties don't allow tiny houses as permanent dwellings
  • Depreciation: Tiny houses on wheels depreciate like RVs — 20% in year one
  • Insurance: Harder to insure as a primary residence

Durability Comparison

Tiny houses are built with wood framing — the same construction as traditional homes, just smaller. They're vulnerable to rot, termites, mold, and weather damage. A typical tiny house lasts 15-30 years with proper maintenance.

Container homes are built from 14-gauge corten steel — the same material used in ocean shipping containers that survive decades at sea. They're Category 4 hurricane rated, seismic Zone 4 earthquake rated, fire resistant, and pest proof. A container home lasts 50+ years with minimal maintenance.

Build Time Comparison

A custom tiny house from a builder takes 4 to 8 months from deposit to delivery. DIY builds can take a year or more. You're waiting on contractors, weather, and permits.

An ATV Homes container home ships in 45 days from order. Our team assembles it on your property in 2-7 days. You're not waiting on anything — you place the order and it arrives.

Zoning & Legality

This is where tiny houses struggle the most. Many municipalities classify tiny houses on wheels as RVs, which means you can't legally live in them full-time on your own land. Tiny house zoning is a patchwork of restrictions across the country.

Container homes are classified as prefabricated structures under IBC Section 3102. Most counties permit them the same as modular homes. We provide stamped engineering drawings approved for all 50 states.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose a tiny house if: You want to build it yourself, you need portability (wheels), and you have the budget ($40K+) and time (6+ months).

Choose a container home if: You want the most affordable option, you need it delivered and assembled fast, you want maximum durability, and you want a permanent structure that will appreciate in value.
