April 24, 2026 · 7 min read

Every container home needs a solid foundation. The good news: container homes are structural steel boxes that can sit on simpler foundations than traditional wood-frame houses.

Concrete Piers ($600-$1,200) - Most Popular

Six to eight concrete piers placed at load-bearing points. Fast, cheap, meets most building codes. ATV Homes recommends this for 80% of customers.

Gravel Pad ($400-$900) - Budget Option

Compacted gravel base 6-12 inches deep. Great for workshops, ADUs, or seasonal homes. Not recommended for permanent residences in freeze-thaw climates.

Concrete Slab ($2,500-$5,000) - Best for Cold Climates

4-inch reinforced concrete. Prevents frost heave. Great for radiant floor heating. Adds about $3K to build cost but increases resale value.
