Working Holiday Visa Eligibility 2026

The Working Holiday Visa (Subclass 417) and Work and Holiday Visa (Subclass 462) allow young adults to work and travel in Australia. Requirements vary slightly between the two subclasses.

Subclass 417 Eligibility

  • Age: 18-35 years (inclusive)
  • Countries: UK, Canada, France, Germany, Ireland, Italy, Netherlands, Sweden, and 10+ others
  • Funds: AUD $5,000+ in savings
  • Health: Meet health requirements
  • Character: No substantial criminal record

Subclass 462 Eligibility

  • Age: 18-30 years (inclusive)
  • Countries: USA, Chile, Indonesia, Thailand, Turkey, and others
  • Education: Minimum of 2 years tertiary education
  • Funds: AUD $5,000+ in savings
  • English: Functional English required

Step-by-Step Application Process

1. Create ImmiAccount

Visit the official Australian Government ImmiAccount portal and create your account. This is where you'll submit your application.

Required: Overseas Visitor Health Cover (OVHC)

Working Holiday Visa requires specific health insurance. Compare OVHC policies from trusted providers.

Get OVHC Insurance

2. Gather Required Documents

  • Passport: Valid for entire stay + 6 months
  • Bank statements: Showing AUD $5,000+
  • Passport photos: Recent, passport-sized
  • Character documents: Police certificates if required
  • Health documents: Medical examination results

3. Complete Online Application

Fill out the online form accurately. Common mistakes include incorrect passport details, insufficient funds evidence, and incomplete health declarations.

4. Pay Application Fee & Submit

The current visa application charge is AUD $635 (2026). Payment is non-refundable even if your application is rejected.

5. Wait for Decision

Current processing times (2026): 2-8 weeks. You may be asked for additional information during processing.

Finding Jobs in Australia on WHV

Popular WHV Job Industries

Hospitality

Cafes, restaurants, bars, hotels. Best in cities and tourist areas. Average wage: AUD $25-$35/hour.

Farming & Agriculture

Fruit picking, farm work for second-year visa. Regional areas. Average wage: AUD $22-$28/hour + piece rates.

Office & Admin

Temporary roles in cities. Reception, data entry, customer service. Average wage: AUD $25-$30/hour.

Ski Resorts

Winter jobs in NSW and Victoria mountains. June-October season. Average wage: AUD $25-$35/hour + accommodation.

Getting Your Tax File Number (TFN)

A TFN is required for employment in Australia. Apply online through the Australian Taxation Office (ATO):

  1. Visit the ATO website
  2. Complete the online TFN application for foreigners
  3. You'll need your passport and Australian address
  4. TFN arrives by mail in 10-28 days

Important Tax Information

WHV holders pay tax like Australian residents but don't get the tax-free threshold for the first 6 months. You'll pay tax from your first dollar earned. Tax rates: 0-18,200: 0%, 18,201-45,000: 19%, 45,001-120,000: 32.5%.

Second & Third Year Visa

Second-Year Visa Requirements

To qualify for a second Working Holiday Visa, you must complete 3 months (88 days) of specified work in regional Australia:

  • Eligible work: Plant and animal cultivation, fishing and pearling, tree farming, mining, construction
  • Regional areas: Most of Australia except major cities
  • Documentation: Keep payslips, bank statements, employer references
  • Application: Apply from within Australia before first visa expires

Third-Year Visa (New 2026 Option)

From 2026, a third-year WHV is available if you complete 6 months of specified work during your second year.

Pro Tips & Common Mistakes

Arrival Tips

  • Arrive during Australian summer (Nov-Feb) for most job opportunities
  • Book first 2 weeks accommodation in advance
  • Get Australian SIM card at airport (Telstra best coverage)

Money Tips

  • Bring AUD $3,000-$4,000 cash for initial setup
  • Use Wise (TransferWise) for best exchange rates
  • Keep detailed records for tax return

Accommodation Tips

  • Hostels for first month while job hunting
  • Flatmate.com.au for shared housing
  • Negotiate rent inclusive of bills

Avoid These Mistakes

  • Don't work for cash-in-hand (no tax, no second-year proof)
  • Don't overstay your visa (3-year ban)
  • Don't forget to apply for TFN before working
