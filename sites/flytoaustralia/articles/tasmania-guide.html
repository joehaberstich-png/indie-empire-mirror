Tasmania: Australia's Island Wilderness - Complete Travel Guide

Updated April 2026 | 22 min read

Tasmania: Australia's Natural Treasure

Tasmania is Australia's island state, separated from the mainland by Bass Strait. Known for its pristine wilderness, unique wildlife, cool-climate wines, and fresh produce, Tasmania offers a completely different Australian experience.

Quick Facts: Population: 541,000 • Capital: Hobart • Size: 68,401 km² • UNESCO Sites: 5 • National Parks: 19

Why Visit Tasmania?

  • Wilderness: Over 40% of Tasmania is protected as national parks and reserves
  • Wildlife: See Tasmanian devils, wombats, platypus, and unique birdlife
  • Food & Wine: Renowned for cool-climate wines, cheese, seafood, and whisky
  • Hiking: World-class multi-day hikes including Overland Track
  • History: Convict heritage sites and well-preserved colonial architecture
  • Art: MONA (Museum of Old and New Art) - Australia's largest private museum

Hobart: Tasmania's Charming Capital

Hobart is Australia's second-oldest capital city (founded 1804) and combines historic charm with contemporary culture against the backdrop of Mount Wellington.

Top Hobart Attractions

MONA (Museum of Old and New Art)

Australia's largest private museum, built into a cliff on the Derwent River. Known for provocative contemporary art.

  • Getting there: MONA Roma ferry from Brooke Street Pier (30 minutes)
  • Highlights: Cloaca Professional (poo machine), Bit.Fall, death gallery
  • Events: Dark Mofo winter festival (June), MONA FOMA summer festival
  • Admission: AUD$35 adults, free for Tasmanians
Visit MONA Website

Mount Wellington (Kunanyi)

1,271m mountain overlooking Hobart with panoramic views and alpine environment.

  • Access: 30-minute drive from CBD, or hiking trails
  • Viewing platform: 360-degree views (often windy and cold)
  • Hiking: Organ Pipes track, Zig Zag track, Springs to Summit
  • Weather: Can be 10-15°C colder than Hobart - bring warm clothes

Salamanca Market

Saturday market (8:30am-3pm) with 300+ stalls selling local produce, crafts, and food.

  • Location: Salamanca Place (historic sandstone warehouses)
  • Best for: Local crafts, fresh produce, street food
  • Must-try: Tasmanian scallop pies, leatherwood honey, whisky fudge
  • Tip: Arrive early (by 9am) to avoid crowds

Port Arthur Historic Site

Australia's best-preserved convict settlement (90-minute drive from Hobart).

  • History: Operated 1833-1877 as penal settlement
  • Tours: Included in admission: guided tour, harbor cruise
  • Ghost tours: Night-time tours available (additional cost)
  • Admission: AUD$47 adults, valid for 2 consecutive days
Book Port Arthur Tickets

Hobart Food & Drink

  • Franklin Wharf: Fish and chips with waterfront views
  • Battery Point: Historic area with cafes and restaurants
  • Cascade Brewery: Australia's oldest operating brewery (tours available)
  • Whisky: Sullivan's Cove (World's Best Single Malt 2014), Lark Distillery
  • Farm Gate Market: Sunday market focusing on local producers

Tasmania's East Coast

The East Coast is known for white sand beaches, crystal-clear water, and coastal national parks.

Freycinet National Park & Wineglass Bay

One of Tasmania's most iconic destinations, famous for the curved white beach of Wineglass Bay.

Wineglass Bay Lookout Walk

  • Distance: 3km return to lookout, 6km return to beach
  • Duration: 1-1.5 hours to lookout, 2.5-3 hours to beach
  • Difficulty: Moderate (steep sections, steps)
  • Best time: Early morning for photography and fewer people
  • Park entry: AUD$44.75 per vehicle (24-hour pass)

Other Freycinet Walks

  • Hazards Beach Circuit: 11km loop combining Wineglass Bay and Hazards Beach
  • Sleepy Bay & Cape Tourville: Easy walks with coastal views
  • Mount Amos: Challenging 3-4 hour climb for panoramic views (experienced hikers only)

Freycinet Accommodation

Camping
  • National park campgrounds: Richardsons Beach, Ranger Creek
  • Facilities: Basic (toilets, water), no power
  • Booking: Essential during peak season
  • Cost: AUD$16 per site per night
Luxury Stays
  • Saffire Freycinet: Ultra-luxury lodge with private suites
  • Freycinet Lodge: Cabins within the national park
  • Edge of the Bay: Resort with beachfront cabins
Book Freycinet Accommodation

Bay of Fires

Stretching from Binalong Bay to Eddystone Point, named for the orange lichen-covered granite boulders.

  • Best beaches: Binalong Bay, The Gardens, Cosy Corner
  • Activities: Swimming, kayaking, fishing, camping
  • Camping: Free camping at designated areas (bring own water)
  • Multi-day walk: Bay of Fires Lodge Walk (4-day guided walk)

Maria Island

Car-free national park island accessible by ferry from Triabunna (1.5 hours from Hobart).

  • Wildlife: Tasmanian devils, wombats, Cape Barren geese, Forester kangaroos
  • History: Convict settlement and industrial ruins
  • Accommodation: Camping or basic bunkhouse (book through Parks Tasmania)
  • Ferry: Maria Island Ferry from Triabunna (AUD$50 return)

Cradle Mountain-Lake St Clair National Park

Part of the Tasmanian Wilderness World Heritage Area, featuring ancient rainforests, alpine moorlands, and glacial lakes.

Dove Lake Circuit

  • Distance: 6km loop
  • Duration: 2-3 hours
  • Difficulty: Easy to moderate (boardwalk and gravel paths)
  • Highlights: Boat shed, Glacier Rock, views of Cradle Mountain
  • Best time: Early morning for reflections on the lake

Overland Track

Australia's premier multi-day alpine walk, covering 65km from Cradle Mountain to Lake St Clair.

Important: The Overland Track requires planning, fitness, and appropriate gear. Hikers must be self-sufficient.

Cradle Mountain Huts Walk

Luxury guided walk with private huts, hot showers, and gourmet meals.

  • Duration: 6 days
  • Cost: AUD$4,995 per person
  • Includes: All meals, wine, accommodation, guides
  • Group size: Maximum 10 guests
Book Cradle Mountain Huts

Other Cradle Mountain Walks

  • Crater Lake Circuit: 5.7km, 2 hours, moderate
  • Marions Lookout: 6km return, 3 hours, challenging
  • Enchanted Walk: 1.1km, 20 minutes, easy (great for families)
  • Weindorfers Forest Walk: 1.5km, 30 minutes, easy rainforest walk

Wildlife at Cradle Mountain

  • Tasmanian devils: Join a night feeding tour at Devils@Cradle sanctuary
  • Wombats: Commonly seen grazing near Ronny Creek in late afternoon
  • Platypus: Look in still pools at dawn or dusk
  • Birds: Black currawongs, green rosellas, yellow wattlebirds
