Sydney Overview

Sydney, Australia's largest and most famous city, is a vibrant metropolis known for its stunning harbor, iconic landmarks, and beautiful beaches. With a population of over 5 million, Sydney offers a perfect blend of urban sophistication and natural beauty.

Quick Facts

  • Population: 5.3 million
  • Best time to visit: September-November, March-May
  • Currency: Australian Dollar (AUD)
  • Language: English
  • Time zone: AEST (UTC+10)

Top Attractions in Sydney

Sydney Opera House

The UNESCO World Heritage-listed Sydney Opera House is Australia's most recognizable landmark. Designed by Danish architect Jørn Utzon, it opened in 1973 and hosts over 1,500 performances annually.

Book Opera House Tours

Skip the lines and book your Opera House tour in advance. We recommend Viator for the best prices and availability.

Book Opera House Tour

Sydney Harbour Bridge

Affectionately known as "The Coathanger," the Sydney Harbour Bridge offers spectacular views of the city. For the adventurous, the BridgeClimb experience is a must-do.

Bondi Beach

Australia's most famous beach, Bondi is perfect for swimming, surfing, or just relaxing. Don't miss the Bondi to Coogee coastal walk for stunning ocean views.

Royal Botanic Garden

Located next to the Opera House, these beautiful gardens offer free admission and spectacular harbor views. The Calyx greenhouse is particularly impressive.

3-Day Sydney Itinerary

Day 1: Iconic Sydney

  • Morning: Sydney Opera House tour
  • Afternoon: Royal Botanic Garden walk
  • Evening: Dinner at Circular Quay with harbor views

Day 2: Beaches & Coastal Walks

  • Morning: Bondi Beach and Bondi Icebergs Pool
  • Afternoon: Bondi to Coogee coastal walk
  • Evening: Dinner in Coogee or return to Bondi

Day 3: Harbour & History

  • Morning: Ferry to Manly Beach
  • Afternoon: The Rocks historic district
  • Evening: BridgeClimb or harbor cruise
