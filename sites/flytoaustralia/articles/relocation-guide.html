Skilled Migration Points Test 2026

You need 65+ points for skilled migration. Points are awarded for:

  • Age: 25-32 years = 30 points (maximum)
  • English: Superior (IELTS 8+) = 20 points
  • Work Experience: 8+ years overseas = 15 points
  • Education: PhD = 20 points, Bachelor = 15 points
  • Australian Study: 2+ years in Australia = 5 points
  • Partner Skills: Partner meets requirements = 10 points

What You Can't Bring to Australia

Strict Biosecurity Laws

Australia has very strict quarantine laws. These items are prohibited or require treatment:

  • Food: Most fresh food, dairy, meat, honey
  • Plants: Soil, seeds, plant material
  • Animal products: Feathers, bones, shells
  • Wooden items: Must be treated if used outdoors
  • Sports equipment: Clean thoroughly (soil/mud)

Always declare questionable items. Fines up to AUD $420,000 for serious breaches.

Rental Application Process

  1. Documentation: Passport, visa, references, proof of income
  2. Bond: Usually 4 weeks rent (held by government)
  3. Advance Rent: 2 weeks rent in advance
  4. Application: Often multiple applicants per property
  5. Condition Report: Document property condition at start

School Zones & Catchment Areas

Important for Public Schools

Public schools in Australia have catchment areas (zones). You must live within the zone to enroll your child. Key points:

  • Check school zones before renting/buying
  • Proof of address required (lease agreement, utility bills)
  • Popular schools may have waiting lists
  • Some states allow out-of-zone enrollment if spaces available

International Student Considerations

If moving with school-age children on temporary visas:

  • Public schools: May charge fees for temporary visa holders ($5,000-$15,000/year)
  • Private schools: Higher fees but may have more international student support
  • English support: Most schools offer ESL (English as Second Language) programs
  • Documentation: Bring school records, vaccination records, birth certificates

Healthcare System in Australia

Medicare: Australia's Public Health System

Eligibility

Australian citizens, permanent residents, and some temporary residents. Apply after arriving in Australia.

What's Covered

Doctor visits, public hospital treatment, some tests. Does NOT cover dental, optical, ambulance (varies by state).

Costs

Funded by 2% Medicare levy on income. Bulk billing doctors = no out-of-pocket cost.
