5 Signs You Need an Ingrown Toenail Correction

April 24, 2026 · 5 min read

OnyfixFoot CareNail Health

Ingrown toenails are one of the most common foot problems we see at Jeannie Nails. They occur when the edge of your toenail grows into the surrounding skin, causing pain, redness, and sometimes infection. Left untreated, they can become a recurring problem that affects your daily life.

The good news: you don't need surgery. At our Sheet Harbour salon, we use the Onyfix system — a medical-grade brace that gently lifts the nail and corrects its growth pattern. Here are the five signs it's time to book a correction.

1. Persistent Pain at the Nail Edge

If the corner of your toenail hurts when you press on it or when you wear closed-toe shoes, it's likely ingrown. Early-stage ingrown nails may only hurt when pressure is applied, but over time the pain becomes constant. Don't wait until walking hurts.

2. Redness and Swelling

Inflammation around the nail bed is your body's natural response to the nail digging into the skin. If you notice the skin around your nail is red, warm to the touch, or slightly swollen, that's a clear sign the nail is irritating the tissue. This is your cue to act before it gets worse.

3. You Keep Cutting the Same Side

If you find yourself constantly trimming the corner of the same nail trying to relieve pressure, stop. Repeated cutting actually makes ingrown nails worse — it encourages the nail to grow deeper into the skin rather than straight out. The Onyfix brace retrains the nail to grow properly over 6-8 months.

4. Fluid or Discharge

When an ingrown nail breaks the skin, bacteria can enter and cause infection. Yellow or clear fluid around the nail is a sign of early infection. At this stage, treatment is still non-surgical, but prompt attention is important to prevent the infection from spreading.

5. Previous Ingrown Nails

If you've had an ingrown toenail before, you're likely to get it again. The nail shape that caused the problem doesn't change on its own. Onyfix provides a permanent correction by applying gentle, continuous pressure that reshapes the nail as it grows.

How Onyfix Works

Onyfix is a clinically proven, non-invasive treatment that applies a small brace to the surface of the nail. The brace is custom-fitted to your nail shape and applies gentle upward pressure at the edges where the nail curves inward. As your nail grows, it follows the corrected path.

The treatment takes about 30 minutes in our salon. Most clients feel relief within 24 hours. The brace stays on through normal activities including showering and exercise, and is adjusted at follow-up visits every 6-8 weeks until the nail grows out fully corrected.

Prevention Tips

  • Trim nails straight across, never curved at the corners
  • Wear shoes with adequate toe room — tight shoes are a leading cause
  • Keep feet clean and dry
  • Moisturize cuticles regularly
  • See a professional if you notice early signs
