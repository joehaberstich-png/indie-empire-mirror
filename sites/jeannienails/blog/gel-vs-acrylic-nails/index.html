One of the most common questions we hear from new clients at Jeannie Nails is whether they should get gel or acrylic nails. Both have their strengths and the right choice depends on your lifestyle, nail type, and personal goals. Here is an honest comparison to help you decide.

When to Choose Gel

Gel is the better choice for most of our clients. UV gel is more flexible than acrylic which means it moves with your natural nail instead of against it. This flexibility reduces breakage and allows your natural nails to grow longer underneath the enhancement. Gel also has a natural high-gloss finish that does not require buffing. It looks like your own nails only better. Removal is also easier and safer. Gel soaks off in acetone while acrylic requires filing which can accidentally thin your natural nail plate if not done carefully.

Gel is ideal if you want a natural look, have active hands from typing or working, care about long-term nail health, or prefer a gentler removal process. Many clients tell us their natural nails are actually stronger and longer after switching from acrylic to gel.

When to Choose Acrylic

Acrylic is the better choice when you need maximum durability. If you are hard on your hands or want significant length acrylic provides a stronger structure that resists breaking. Acrylic is also easier to repair if a nail breaks because your technician can fill the break without removing the entire set. Acrylic is best for clients who want very long stiletto or coffin shaped nails, work in jobs that are tough on hands, or tend to break gel nails within a week or two.

Our Professional Recommendation

For most clients we recommend starting with gel. It is healthier for your natural nails, looks more natural, and removal is far gentler on your nail bed. If you find that gel does not last long enough for your lifestyle you can always switch to acrylic on your next visit without any issues. Many clients keep both options available choosing gel for everyday wear and acrylic for special events or vacations when they want maximum durability.
