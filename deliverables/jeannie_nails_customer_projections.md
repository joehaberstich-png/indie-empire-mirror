# Jeannie Nails — Customer Projections

**Location:** Sheet Harbour, Nova Scotia  
**Population:** ~1,000 (town) / ~3,500 (catchment area)  
**Competition:** None within 45 minutes (local monopoly)  
**Hours:** 38/week (Tue–Fri 10–6, Sat 10–4)

---

## Market Context

Sheet Harbour has **zero nail salons**. The nearest is in Porters Lake (45 min) or Halifax (1hr 15min). This creates a natural monopoly on local demand. The catchment also includes seasonal tourism (cottages, camping, hunting) which adds a 15–25% volume boost June–September.

## Capacity Ceiling (Single Operator)

| Factor | Max | Notes |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Appointments per day | 10–12 | 38h/week, blended average 35min/service |
| Customers per week | 50–60 | Realistic with cancellations & gaps |
| Customers per month | 200–250 | ~85% capacity utilization |
| Max monthly revenue (1 person) | ~$12,500 | At 250 customers × $50 avg |
| **Yearly max (1 person)** | **~$150,000** | Solo ceiling |

To exceed $150,000/yr, a **2nd chair / employee** is needed.

---

## Key Drivers for High Projection

1. **Monopoly position** — zero competition in Sheet Harbour
2. **Onyfix specialty** — ingrown toenail brace draws from Eastern Shore + HRM (medical need drives longer drive times)
3. **Tootsie Tuesday** — creates weekly recurring traffic on slowest day
4. **Seasonal tourism** — June–September adds 15–25% volume (cottages, campgrounds)
5. **80+ service categories** — every customer segment has an entry point ($5 polish change to $125 waxing combo)
6. **Online booking + blog SEO** — captures Google searches for "nail salon Sheet Harbour"
